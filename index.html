History of April Fools Day

April Fools' Day has roots in European history, especially around calendar changes in the 16th century. One popular theory says people who continued celebrating the new year around late March were mocked after the shift to January 1.

Over time, playful tricks and harmless hoaxes on April 1 became a yearly custom in many places.

Countries that Celebrates April Fools Day

April Fools' Day is commonly celebrated in countries such as the United States, Canada, the United Kingdom, France, Germany, Italy, and Australia.

Traditions vary, but lighthearted practical jokes and fake news stories are common themes across many cultures.
